For nearly two decades, YPP has served as a national platform for nurturing leadership, promoting democratic values, encouraging civic participation, and developing responsible citizens who are committed to public service and national progress.

The story of Youth Parliament of Pakistan is ultimately a story of hope. Youth Parliament of Pakistan 

Youth Parliament of Pakistan is conceived, conceptualized and planned to execute with a
 vision to empower the youth of Pakistan with the ability to understand the importance of their role in community 
services and to nurture their leadership qualities. YPP is a non- profit, non-political, non-religious program 
initiated to foster and translate talent and excellence of adolescents and youths of Pakistan into tangible action and 
community service and in the process provide a nursery for scientifically trained leaders and citizens of character 
and substance. In the process the community’s abandoned,sick and underprivileged are linked and benefited in a 
relationship of symbiosis and interdependence with the youth of Pakistan. Youth Parliament of Pakistan is conceived, 
conceptualized and planned to execute with a vision to empower the people of Pakistan focusing on youth with the ability 
to understand the importance of their role in community services and to nurture their leadership qualities to pave the way for establishment of a democratic society. 




Youth Parliament of Pakistan works in the following thematic areas; 

1- Youth Parliament of Pakistan works across the following strategic thematic areas designed to promote democratic governance, youth empowerment, inclusive development, and global engagement. 

 


 

2- Democratic Governance & Civic Participation Promoting democratic values, constitutional awareness, civic engagement, and active citizenship among young people through parliamentary simulations, policy dialogue, and institutional learning.

Following are the aims of Youth Parliament of Pakistan; 

 

The Youth Parliament of Pakistan aims to empower young people as responsible citizens, ethical leaders, and active contributors to national and global development through the following objectives:

1- To promote democratic values, constitutional awareness, and active citizenship among young people. 

 

2- To encourage youth engagement in policymaking, social innovation, and evidence-based advocacy. 

 

3- To support youth participation in achieving the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), particularly in education, equality, climate action, and peacebuilding. 

 

4- To promote digital citizenship, responsible use of technology, and awareness of misinformation in the digital age. 

 

5- To develop a sense of national unity while encouraging global understanding, international cooperation, and cross-cultural dialogue. 

 

6- To empower youth as change makers who contribute to building a peaceful, progressive, and inclusive Pakistan.

Title: KON HO GA NOJAWANO KA WAZIR-E-AZAM 

Broadcast Partner: ATV (Pakistan) 

“Kon Ho Ga Nojawano Ka Wazir-e-Azam” is a flagship youth leadership and political awareness programme of Youth Parliament of Pakistan, aired on ATV. The programme is designed to engage young people in constructive dialogue on governance, leadership, national issues, and democratic participation. 

Through interactive discussions, youth debates, and leadership challenges, the programme provides a platform for young participants to express their ideas, present solutions to national challenges, and develop critical thinking and communication skills. 

The initiative reflects YPP’s commitment to promoting democratic values, civic awareness, and youth engagement in mainstream media, while encouraging responsible and solution-oriented discourse among young citizens. 

The programme has played an important role in bridging the gap between youth and decision-making processes by giving them visibility in national media and encouraging them to think like future leaders. 

Prgram Link : https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aigmJvDZijo 

2- 

Title: FLOOD REHABILITATION AND HOUSING RECONSTRUCTION PROGRAMME

Partner: Organization of Islamic Cooperation (OIC) 

Following the devastating floods that struck Pakistan in 2010, causing widespread destruction and displacement, Youth Parliament of Pakistan actively participated in one of the country's significant post-disaster rehabilitation efforts. 

With the support of the Organization of Islamic Cooperation (OIC), YPP contributed to the reconstruction and rehabilitation of flood-affected communities in Sindh and Punjab. 

Under this initiative, approximately 1,400 houses were constructed for vulnerable families in the severely affected districts of Dadu and Larkana in Sindh and Muzaffargarh in Punjab. 

The programme restored shelter, dignity, and hope to thousands of flood survivors who had lost their homes and livelihoods. 

The initiative stands as one of the most significant humanitarian contributions associated with Youth Parliament of Pakistan and demonstrated the organization's commitment to serving communities during times of crisis. 

In recognition of these humanitarian efforts and contributions toward disaster recovery, Chairman Abrar-ul-Haq was honoured by the Organization of Islamic Cooperation and designated as its first Humanitarian Ambassador. 

Key Achievements 

• 1,400 houses constructed 
• Thousands of flood survivors supported 
• Rehabilitation support in Sindh and Punjab 
• Strong partnership with international humanitarian actors 
• Long-term community recovery facilitated 

3- 

Title: KNOW YOUR RIGHTS | HUMAN RIGHTS EDUCATION PROGRAMME

Partner: The Swedish Internatinal Development Cooperation Agency (S.I.D.A) 

The Know Your Rights programme was launched to promote awareness and understanding of fundamental human rights among young people and local communities across Pakistan. 

The initiative focused on educating citizens about the principles and values enshrined in the Universal Declaration of Human Rights (UDHR), encouraging greater awareness of rights, responsibilities, equality, dignity, and social justice. 

Through workshops, seminars, community dialogues, awareness sessions, and educational outreach activities, the programme engaged students, youth leaders, educators, and community representatives in meaningful discussions on human rights issues. 

The project contributed to strengthening civic awareness, promoting tolerance and inclusion, and encouraging active participation in building a more equitable and rights-respecting society. 

Project Documentary Link: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z8gNdpuSOAI 

4- 

Title: YOUTH ACTION FOR DEMOCRACY (2013) 

Donor: United Nations Democracy Fund (UNDEF) 
Partner: Election Commission of Pakistan 

Youth Action for Democracy was a nationwide initiative aimed at strengthening democratic participation and electoral awareness among young people ahead of Pakistan's historic democratic transition. The project sought to increase youth engagement in democratic processes and encourage informed participation in elections. 

Working closely with the Election Commission of Pakistan, Youth Parliament of Pakistan conducted awareness campaigns, training sessions, seminars, and voter education activities across multiple regions of the country. 

The programme focused on educating young citizens about voter registration procedures, electoral processes, polling mechanisms, and the roles and responsibilities of election officials. 

Through this initiative, approximately 500,000 new young voters were mobilized and registered, contributing to increased youth participation in Pakistan's democratic process. 

Key Achievements 

• Approximately 500,000 youth voters mobilized 
• Nationwide voter education campaigns 
• Electoral awareness workshops conducted 
• Strengthened youth participation in democratic processes 
• Partnership established with Election Commission of Pakistan 

Project Detail Link: https://www.undeflessonslearned.org/index.php/projects/youth-action-democracy-yad-strengthening-democracy-empowering-youth-pakistan 

5- 

Title: UMEED-E-JAWAN (2015) 

Donor: USAID 

Umeed-e-Jawan was a youth empowerment and vocational skills development initiative implemented in Southern Punjab. 

The project operated through Youth Resource Centres in Bahawalpur, Lodhran, Multan, and Muzaffargarh, providing young people, particularly women and vulnerable groups, with opportunities for skills development, economic participation, and self-reliance. 

Vocational training programmes focused on embroidery, stitching, and income-generation skills designed to improve livelihoods and create pathways toward economic empowerment. 

The project culminated in the Southern Punjab Youth Festival held in Multan on 16 April 2015, bringing together beneficiaries from 11 Youth Resource Centres. More than 400 participants and guests attended the event, showcasing vocational products and celebrating youth achievements. 

Key Achievements 

• Four districts covered 
• 11 Youth Resource Centres established 
• Hundreds of youth trained 
• Vocational and entrepreneurship skills developed 
• Southern Punjab Youth Festival successfully organized 
• More than 400 participants engaged

Title: ACTIVE CITIZENS OF PAKISTAN 

Donor: British Council 

The Active Citizens of Pakistan initiative was implemented in partnership with the British Council to strengthen youth leadership, advocacy skills, community engagement, and social responsibility among young people across Pakistan. 

Through a series of intensive five-day capacity-building workshops, participants were equipped with practical skills in leadership, advocacy, project management, communication, networking, conflict resolution, and community mobilization. The programme encouraged young people to identify social issues within their communities and develop sustainable solutions. 

A key component of the programme was the Social Action Project (SAP) model, through which participants designed and implemented community-based interventions addressing local development challenges. 

As a result of this initiative, members of the Youth Parliament of Pakistan successfully implemented 127 Social Action Projects across Pakistan, including Gilgit-Baltistan and Azad Jammu & Kashmir. 

These projects addressed a wide range of development issues including: 

• Education and literacy 
• Public health awareness 
• Child rights protection 
• Elimination of child labour 
• Women empowerment 
• Water, sanitation and hygiene (WASH) 
• Environmental protection 
• Community development 
• Youth engagement and volunteerism 

The programme contributed significantly to strengthening youth-led development and community resilience by enabling young people to become agents of positive change within their local communities. 

Key Achievements 

• 127 Social Action Projects implemented 
• Activities conducted across all provinces, GB and AJK 
• Hundreds of youth leaders trained 
• Thousands of direct and indirect beneficiaries reached 
• Sustainable community engagement initiatives established
